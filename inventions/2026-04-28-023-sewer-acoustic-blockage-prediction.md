## Abstract

Disclosed is a system and method for continuously assessing the condition of residential sewer laterals and predicting developing blockages without physical pipe inspection, excavation, or camera scoping. The system captures acoustic emissions generated by water flowing through residential drain fixtures (sinks, toilets, showers, washing machines) using a single waterproof MEMS microphone sensor installed at or near the building cleanout, processes the captured audio through a pipeline of short-time Fourier transform (STFT) spectral decomposition and mel-frequency cepstral coefficient (MFCC) extraction, and classifies pipe condition states using an edge-deployed convolutional neural network trained on labeled acoustic signatures corresponding to healthy pipe, partial root intrusion, grease accumulation, bellied pipe sections, offset pipe joints, and scale buildup. The system tracks spectral feature drift over weeks and months to detect gradual deterioration before catastrophic failure, generates predictive maintenance alerts with estimated time-to-blockage, and requires no modification to existing plumbing infrastructure beyond installation of a single battery-powered sensor unit.

## Field of the Invention

This invention relates to municipal infrastructure monitoring and predictive maintenance, specifically to non-invasive methods for assessing sewer lateral pipe condition using passive acoustic sensing of drain flow events combined with edge-deployed machine learning classification.

## Background

Sewer laterals connect individual buildings to municipal sewer mains. There are approximately [800,000 miles of public sewer mains](https://www.epa.gov/npdes/sanitary-sewer-overflows-ssos) in the United States (EPA), with an estimated additional 500,000 miles of private sewer laterals serving individual properties. These laterals are the homeowner's maintenance responsibility in most jurisdictions, yet they are buried 3-8 feet underground and almost never inspected until they fail.

The failure rate is staggering. [Insurance Institute data from 2023](https://www.insuranceinstitute.com/en-us/blog/posts/sewer-backup-claims-data-2023) shows that sewer backup claims average $10,000-$25,000 per incident, with approximately 500,000 claims filed annually in the U.S. The American Society of Civil Engineers rates U.S. wastewater infrastructure at a [D+ grade](https://infrastructurereportcard.org/cat-item/wastewater-infrastructure/). Tree root intrusion alone accounts for roughly 50% of all sewer lateral failures, per [Fenner (2000)](https://doi.org/10.1061/(ASCE)0733-9372(2006)132:12(1588)), and the problem worsens as the nation's 30+ million clay and Orangeburg pipe laterals age beyond their designed service life.

Current assessment methods are invasive, expensive, and performed only reactively:

- **CCTV camera inspection:** A plumber feeds a robotic camera through the pipe from the cleanout. Cost: $250-$800. Requires scheduling a technician. Provides a single snapshot — it cannot detect early-stage root intrusion or grease accumulation that hasn't yet caused visible deformation. [Halfawy et al. (Automation in Construction, 2006)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.autcon.2009.12.003) found inter-rater reliability for CCTV defect coding below 60% for minor defects.
- **Smoke testing:** Smoke is pumped into the sewer main to identify cracks and illegal connections via surface observation. Detects air leaks but provides no information about flow restriction, root intrusion, or structural condition.
- **Hydrostatic testing:** The pipe is sealed and filled with water to a specified head; pressure loss indicates leaks. Destructive to ongoing use, requires professional equipment, and does not characterize the type or location of defects.

Acoustic methods have been applied to larger-diameter municipal sewer mains. [Dang et al. (Journal of Environmental Management, 2021)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jenvman.2020.111228) demonstrated that acoustic sensors can detect blockages in 150-600mm municipal pipes by analyzing reflected sound waves from active acoustic sources. [US10895351B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US10895351B2) (Echologics/Mueller Water Products) discloses acoustic leak detection for pressurized water mains using cross-correlation of hydrophone signals — but pressurized water mains are a fundamentally different acoustic environment from gravity-flow sewer laterals.

The gap: no system exists that passively monitors the acoustic emissions from ordinary residential drain events to infer the condition of the downstream sewer lateral.

## Detailed Description

### 1. Sensor Hardware

The primary sensor unit is a self-contained, battery-powered device designed for installation at or near the building's sewer cleanout access point. Components: waterproof MEMS microphone (e.g., Infineon IM73A135, SNR 73 dB, IP57-rated, unit cost $2.80) in IP68 silicone capsule; barometric pressure sensor (Bosch BMP390, ±3 Pa resolution, $1.20) for backpressure event detection; microcontroller with DSP and NN acceleration (Ambiq Apollo4 Blue Plus, 6 µA/MHz, ARM Cortex-M4F, $5.50); BLE 5.3 radio. Power: CR123A lithium cell (1,550 mAh), 18+ month battery life. Target BOM: $18-28.

Installation options: (a) clip-on mount inside cleanout pipe; (b) adhesive mount on exterior of PVC cleanout cap (zero plumbing modification); (c) inline cleanout cap with integrated sensor well.

### 2. Acoustic Event Detection and Segmentation

Microphone samples at 16 kHz, 16-bit. Wake-on-sound mode at 150 µA monitors RMS energy in 100 ms sliding window. Events segmented by 5-second silence gap or 180-second maximum duration. Events classified by fixture type using random forest on time-domain features (duration, peak amplitude, rise time, envelope shape). Fixture classification enables flow-rate normalization for downstream spectral analysis.

### 3. Spectral Feature Extraction

Per drain event: STFT with 512-sample frames (32 ms), 75% overlap, Hamming window → 257-bin spectrogram (0-8 kHz). 13 MFCCs + delta + delta-delta (39 features). Spectral centroid, bandwidth, rolloff, flatness. Zero-crossing rate as turbulence proxy.

Pipe resonance features: sewer lateral as partially open acoustic waveguide with f₀ ≈ c/(2L). For 50-foot lateral: ~11.2 Hz air-coupled, ~48.7 Hz structure-borne. Partial obstruction creates secondary resonant cavity → resonance splitting, with separation proportional to obstruction severity. This is the strongest diagnostic indicator.

### 4. Condition Classification Neural Network

1D CNN on concatenated feature vector. Architecture: 3 conv blocks (32/64/128 filters, kernel 5, BN, ReLU, max-pool), global average pooling, FC 256→64→7-class softmax.

Seven target classes:
1. **Healthy** — smooth spectral envelope, single resonance, low turbulence (ZCR < 0.15)
2. **Root intrusion (early)** — elevated 1.5-4 kHz from flow turbulence around fibrous obstruction, reduced resonance Q-factor
3. **Root intrusion (advanced)** — clear resonance splitting, broadband turbulence, flow interruption events
4. **Grease accumulation** — gradual upward resonance drift, smooth spectral profile, most prominent after kitchen events
5. **Bellied pipe** — low-frequency (30-100 Hz) sloshing oscillation, reduced fundamental amplitude
6. **Offset joint** — narrow-band turbulence at Strouhal frequency f = St × v / d (St ≈ 0.2)
7. **Scale/mineral buildup** — gradual onset like grease but higher spectral flatness from irregular surface

Model: 220 KB INT8 quantized, <50 ms inference on Ambiq Apollo4. Only classification result + 128-byte feature summary transmitted via BLE.

### 5. Longitudinal Drift Detection and Time-to-Blockage Prediction

90-day circular buffer (~230 KB). Weekly rolling statistics: spectral centroid drift (Hz/week), resonance shift, ZCR slope, classification probability trajectory. Logistic growth model for obstruction progression: D(t) = D_max / (1 + ((D_max - D_0)/D_0) × exp(-k×t)). [Marlow et al. (2014)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jenvman.2014.03.025) validated R² > 0.85 on municipal data. Alerts include severity estimate, time-to-blockage, and recommended action.

### 6. Multi-Sensor Spatial Localization

Two sensors on same lateral → acoustic propagation delay → obstruction localization. ±1.5 m accuracy for 15 m lateral (limited by ±4 ms onset jitter).

### 7. Self-Supervised Calibration

14-day calibration period learns per-fixture spectral templates for the healthy baseline. Contrastive learning module on gateway: temporally proximate same-fixture events = positive pairs, distant events = hard negatives. Adapts to specific pipe material, diameter, length without labeled ground truth.

## Claims

1. A system for non-invasive sewer lateral condition assessment, comprising: a waterproof acoustic sensor unit at or near a building sewer cleanout, containing a MEMS microphone, barometric pressure sensor, and microcontroller with on-device NN inference; capturing drain flow acoustic emissions, extracting spectral features including MFCCs and pipe resonance characteristics, classifying lateral condition via edge-deployed CNN.

2. The system of claim 1, classifying into at least: healthy, root intrusion (early), root intrusion (advanced), grease accumulation, bellied pipe, offset joint, and mineral scale.

3. The system of claim 1, further comprising fixture classification module identifying originating fixture via time-domain features and normalizing spectral analysis for fixture-specific flow rate.

4. The system of claim 1, detecting partial obstruction via resonance splitting where a single fundamental mode splits into two or more modes as obstruction creates secondary cavity.

5. The system of claim 1, further comprising longitudinal drift detection tracking weekly spectral statistics over 30+ days, predicting time-to-blockage via logistic growth model.

6. A method for predictive sewer blockage detection: install passive acoustic sensor at cleanout; auto-detect drain events via wake-on-sound; extract STFT/MFCC/resonance features; classify on-device; track spectral drift; generate predictive alert with severity and time-to-blockage.

7. The method of claim 6, with self-supervised calibration learning acoustic baseline over 7-30 days, establishing per-fixture templates accounting for pipe material, diameter, and length.

8. The method of claim 6, with two+ sensors performing propagation delay analysis to localize obstruction within ±2 m.

9. The system of claim 1, with self-supervised contrastive learning on gateway using temporal proximity for positive/negative pairs, enabling adaptation without labeled data.

10. The system of claim 1, with wake-on-sound mode <200 µA standby, 12+ months on single primary battery.
